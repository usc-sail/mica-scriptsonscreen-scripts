minute, staring straight at us.

Above the bird, over the image, THERE ARE NUMBERS SHOWING MINUTES,

milliseconds are just a blur on the screen.
The timer nears one minute:
“00:59.991”... “00:59.992”...
TIME SLOWS DOWN. A second now lasts 10 seconds... Everything keeps
slowing down until the bird’s wings become sharper, more visible.

SECOND. The hummingbird seems to float in mid air... ONE SECOND
(one thousand milliseconds...) has elapsed on the timer
(01:00.000)

second... (01:00.001, .002, .003)
A single, isolated hummingbird’s wing flap starts it’s movement,
almost seized in time. It reaches its folded position at ONE
SECOND AND SIXTEEN MILLISECONDS.
“01:00.016”

frozen on the timer, those 16 milliseconds...
